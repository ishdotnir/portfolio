01

Invoice Management

Streamlining invoice creation and management for small business owners on mobile

Context

QuickBooks mobile users were struggling with invoice creation and management, leading to decreased productivity and user satisfaction. Small business owners needed a more intuitive way to handle invoicing on-the-go, as many were managing their businesses primarily from mobile devices.

Challenges

  • Complex invoice creation process with too many steps
  • Poor mobile interface optimization for small screens
  • Inconsistent design patterns across invoice features
  • Limited offline functionality for field workers

Objectives

  • Reduce invoice creation time by 50%
  • Improve mobile user satisfaction scores by 35%
  • Increase mobile invoice completion rates by 40%
  • Create consistent design patterns for financial workflows

The team

  • 2 Product Managers
  • 1 UX Researcher
  • 1 Visual Designer
  • 4 Engineers
  • 1 Data Analyst

My role

Lead UX Designer responsible for mobile interface design, user research, and cross-functional collaboration. I led design workshops, conducted user testing with small business owners, and worked closely with engineering to ensure technical feasibility.

The process

1

User Research & Analysis

Conducted field studies with 28 small business owners, analyzed mobile usage patterns, and identified key pain points in the invoice workflow.

2

Design Strategy

Developed mobile-first design principles, created user journey maps, and established design patterns optimized for touch interfaces and small screens.

3

Prototyping & Testing

Built high-fidelity prototypes and conducted moderated usability tests with 20 business owners to validate design decisions and iterate on solutions.

4

Implementation & Optimization

Collaborated with engineering on technical implementation, created detailed design specifications, and monitored post-launch metrics for continuous improvement.

Final state

The redesigned invoice management system launched with a 55% reduction in creation time and 42% improvement in mobile user satisfaction. The streamlined workflow increased completion rates by 48% and the design patterns were adopted across other QuickBooks mobile features.

-55% Creation time
+48% Completion rates
+42% User satisfaction
02

Inclusive Tax Filing

Making tax preparation accessible for users with visual and cognitive disabilities

Context

TurboTax's accessibility compliance was below industry standards, creating barriers for millions of users with disabilities during tax season. With tax filing being a critical annual requirement, ensuring equal access was both a legal obligation and moral imperative.

Challenges

  • Complex tax forms failed WCAG 2.1 AA compliance standards
  • Screen reader incompatibility with dynamic form elements
  • Overwhelming interface for users with cognitive disabilities
  • Lack of alternative input methods for motor impairments

Objectives

  • Achieve full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance across all tax forms
  • Reduce task completion time for disabled users by 60%
  • Increase tax filing success rates for assistive technology users
  • Create inclusive design standards for all Intuit products

The team

  • 1 Product Manager
  • 1 Accessibility Specialist
  • 1 UX Researcher
  • 3 Engineers
  • 1 QA Tester

My role

Lead UX Designer specializing in inclusive design and accessibility standards. I conducted user research with disabled taxpayers, designed accessible interfaces, and established accessibility guidelines for the broader product team.

The process

1

Accessibility Assessment

Conducted comprehensive accessibility audit using automated tools and manual testing with various assistive technologies to identify compliance gaps.

2

User Research

Interviewed 24 taxpayers with various disabilities, observing their tax filing behaviors and identifying specific barriers with current interface design.

3

Inclusive Redesign

Redesigned tax forms with clear semantic structure, improved navigation, enhanced color contrast, and comprehensive keyboard support throughout the flow.

4

Testing & Validation

Conducted extensive usability testing with disabled users across multiple assistive technologies, iterating based on feedback and compliance validation.

Final state

The redesigned TurboTax achieved full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance and dramatically improved the tax filing experience for users with disabilities. Task completion times decreased by 65% for screen reader users, and the inclusive design principles were adopted across all Intuit products.

100% WCAG AA Compliance
-65% Task completion time
+92% Filing success rate
03

Smart Budgeting

Using behavioral data to create personalized budgeting experiences

Context

Mint users were struggling to create and maintain budgets, with 78% abandoning their budget setup within the first month. Users found generic budget categories irrelevant to their spending patterns and lacked motivation to stick with their financial goals.

Challenges

  • One-size-fits-all budget categories didn't reflect user spending
  • Complex setup process overwhelming new users
  • Lack of personalized insights and recommendations
  • Poor motivation and engagement with budget goals

Objectives

  • Reduce budget abandonment rate from 78% to under 45%
  • Increase budget goal achievement by 60%
  • Improve user engagement with budget features by 50%
  • Create adaptive budget recommendations based on spending behavior

The team

  • 2 Product Managers
  • 1 Data Scientist
  • 1 UX Researcher
  • 4 Engineers
  • 1 Behavioral Economist

My role

Lead UX Designer responsible for personalization strategy, behavioral design patterns, and data-driven interface optimization. I collaborated with data science to create intelligent budget recommendations and designed motivational features to improve user engagement.

The process

1

Behavioral Analysis

Analyzed spending patterns of 50,000+ users to identify common budget categories, success factors, and abandonment triggers using machine learning algorithms.

2

User Interviews

Interviewed 36 Mint users who both succeeded and failed with budgeting to understand motivations, pain points, and behavioral patterns around financial planning.

3

Personalization Design

Designed adaptive budgeting flows with AI-powered category suggestions, personalized spending insights, and behavioral nudges to encourage goal achievement.

4

A/B Testing & Optimization

Ran comprehensive A/B tests on personalization algorithms, interface elements, and motivational features to optimize for long-term user engagement and success.

Final state

The smart budgeting system reduced abandonment rates to 38% and increased goal achievement by 72%. Personalized recommendations led to 65% higher engagement with budget features, and the behavioral design patterns were implemented across other Mint financial planning tools.

-51% Abandonment rate
+72% Goal achievement
+65% Feature engagement
